# Chapter 40: Vengeance Sworn

> *Arc 5: Flowering*

## Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Arc** | Flowering |
| **Perspective** | Hodan (first-person) |
| **Location** | Village ruins, anima tree |
| **Key Events** | Burial rites, the vow, arc conclusion |

## Summary

Cora buries her mother and grandmother, then makes a vow before the anima tree and survivors: she will bring down the Empire. Not just escape it, not just survive it—destroy it. The Flowering Arc ends with a new purpose born from tragedy.

## Frame-by-Frame Description

### Frame 1: Digging
**Visual**: Graves being dug beneath the anima tree.  
**Narration**: "We dig. The earth that Hodan knows so well. Opening to receive our dead."  
**Purpose**: Physical mourning work.

### Frame 2: Ashara's Grave
**Visual**: Lowering her body, gentle, careful.  
**Narration**: "Mother goes into the ground. Back to the earth that fed her family."  
**Purpose**: First burial.

### Frame 3: Willow's Grave
**Visual**: The elder laid beside her student.  
**Narration**: "Grandmother joins her. Teacher and mother, together in the roots."  
**Purpose**: Second burial.

### Frame 4: Prayers
**Visual**: Survivors murmuring old words, ancestral rites.  
**Narration**: "We speak the words they taught us. Returning them to the cycle."  
**Purpose**: Ritual completion.

### Frame 5: Cora Stands
**Visual**: Cora rising from the grave, face set.  
**Narration**: "She stands. Something in her posture has changed. Become... harder."  
**Purpose**: Transformation visible.

### Frame 6: Gathering
**Visual**: Survivors gathering around, waiting.  
**Narration**: "They look to her now. The village has no elder. Cora is what remains."  
**Purpose**: Leadership assumed.

### Frame 7: The Speech
**Visual**: Cora's voice carrying across the ruins.  
**Narration**: "'They came to punish me. They killed OUR people. For following THEIR laws of justice.'"  
**Purpose**: Accusation.

### Frame 8: The System
**Visual**: Cora gesturing at the destruction.  
**Narration**: "'This is what the Empire does. Burns. Kills. Takes. It cannot be reformed.'"  
**Purpose**: Systemic critique.

### Frame 9: The Vow
**Visual**: Cora raising Hodan, light blazing.  
**Narration**: "'I WILL BRING IT DOWN. Not for vengeance. For JUSTICE. For everyone they've murdered.'"  
**Purpose**: Commitment made.

### Frame 10: Hodan's Response
**Visual**: Hodan's glow intensifying in agreement.  
**Narration**: "'We will,' I add through our bond. 'Together. Whatever it takes.'"  
**Purpose**: Partnership affirmed.

### Frame 11: Survivors' Choice
**Visual**: Some villagers stepping forward.  
**Narration**: "'We're with you.' Not all—but enough. A core of the committed."  
**Purpose**: Support gathered.

### Frame 12: Finn's Fate
**Visual**: Cora kneeling to her brother.  
**Narration**: "'I have to go. To fight. I need you to stay. To survive. Can you do that?'"  
**Purpose**: Family decision.

### Frame 13: Finn's Courage
**Visual**: The boy nodding, older than his years.  
**Narration**: "'I'll help rebuild. I'll remember. And when I'm old enough... I'll join you.'"  
**Purpose**: Next generation's promise.

### Frame 14: Departure Preparation
**Visual**: Packing what little remains.  
**Narration**: "No more farming. No more peace. Time to become what they made us: enemies."  
**Purpose**: Transition.

### Frame 15: Looking Forward
**Visual**: Cora walking toward the horizon, Hodan in hand, Chitter following.  
**Narration**: "The flower has bloomed. Not in beauty—in thorns. Time to see what we can destroy."  
**Purpose**: Arc conclusion.
